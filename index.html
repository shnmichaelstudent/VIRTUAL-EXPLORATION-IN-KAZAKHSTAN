About Kazakhstan

Geography

Kazakhstan, the ninth-largest country in the world, spans over 2.7 million square kilometers in Central Asia. It borders Russia, China, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, and Turkmenistan, featuring diverse landscapes such as steppes, deserts, mountains, and lakes.

Languages

Kazakhstan's official languages are Kazakh, a Turkic language, and Russian, widely used in business and government. English is less common, making basic Kazakh or Russian phrases useful for travelers.

Culture

Rooted in nomadic traditions, Kazakh culture values hospitality, with traditional music, dance, and crafts playing a central role. Festivals like Nauryz (New Year) celebrate this rich heritage.

Leisure Activities

Kazakhstan offers diverse activities for outdoor enthusiasts and culture lovers alike:

  • Hiking in the Tian Shan mountains
  • Skiing in Shymbulak
  • Horseback riding on the steppe
  • Visiting museums and art galleries
  • Exploring local markets like the Green Bazaar

Famous Delicacies

Kazakh cuisine reflects its nomadic roots with hearty, meat- and dairy-based dishes:

Beshbarmak: National dish of boiled meat and pasta.

Kazy: Traditional horse meat sausage.

Kumis: Fermented mare's milk, a cultural staple.

Plov: Rice dish with meat and vegetables.

Five Must-Visit Places

Explore these top attractions showcasing Kazakhstan's best:

Almaty

The largest city and cultural hub, nestled against stunning mountains.

Why It's a Top Attraction:

  • Rich history with landmarks like Zenkov Cathedral
  • Vibrant arts scene and bustling markets
  • Nearby outdoor adventures like skiing and hiking

Charyn Canyon

A natural wonder with dramatic landscapes and unique rock formations.

Why It's a Top Attraction:

  • Stunning scenery perfect for photography
  • Hiking and rafting opportunities
  • Chance to spot local wildlife

Lake Kaindy

A surreal lake with a submerged forest and turquoise waters.

Why It's a Top Attraction:

  • Unique underwater forest phenomenon
  • Peaceful setting for hiking and picnics
  • Scuba diving to explore the depths

Astana (Nur-Sultan)

The capital city, known for futuristic architecture and cultural landmarks.

Why It's a Top Attraction:

  • Iconic Bayterek Tower and modern skyline
  • Rich museums and cultural centers
  • Blend of tradition and modernity

Turkestan

A historic city with deep cultural and architectural significance.

Why It's a Top Attraction:

  • UNESCO-listed Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi
  • Ancient Silk Road ruins like Otrar
  • Unique nomadic and urban heritage
